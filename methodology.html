Project Methodology

Experimental Design for Reverse Walking Gait Analysis

A. Participants

Healthy adults between 18 and 30 years of age participated in the study. Participants had no history of neurological disorders, musculoskeletal injury, or balance impairment.

B. Experimental Setup

  • Motion Capture System for measuring joint kinematics
  • Force Plates for ground reaction force measurement
  • Surface EMG Sensors for muscle activation analysis
  • Gastrocnemius muscle selected for EMG analysis

The gastrocnemius muscle was selected because of its important role in gait propulsion and balance.

C. Experimental Conditions

  • Forward Walking (FW)
  • Reverse Walking (RW)
  • Reverse Walking while using Phone (RW+P)

During the dual-task condition, participants walked backward while interacting with a mobile phone.

D. Parameters Measured

  • Walking Speed
  • Step Length
  • Cadence
  • Stance Phase Percentage
  • Swing Phase Percentage
  • Joint Range of Motion
  • Ground Reaction Force
  • Gastrocnemius EMG Activation

E. Data Analysis

Mean values of the recorded parameters were compared across the three walking conditions. Statistical analysis was performed to identify changes in gait mechanics and neuromuscular activity under reverse and dual-task walking conditions.
