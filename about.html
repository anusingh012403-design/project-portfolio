About The Project

Biomechanical and Neuromuscular Adaptations in Reverse Walking

Introduction

Reverse walking is an advanced gait training method used in rehabilitation, sports science and neuromuscular studies. It improves balance, coordination, posture control and lower limb strength.

Problem Statement

There is limited understanding of biomechanical and neuromuscular changes during reverse walking, especially under constrained gait conditions.

Aim

To study how biomechanics (body movement) and muscle activity change during reverse walking under different conditions.

Objectives

  • Measure joint kinematics during reverse walking
  • Analyze muscle activation using surface EMG
  • Compare forward and reverse walking
  • Identify constrained gait adaptations
