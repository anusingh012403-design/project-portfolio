Results & Discussion

Comparative Gait Performance Across Walking Conditions

A. Temporal-Spatial Parameters

Walking speed decreased progressively from forward walking to reverse walking and was lowest during reverse walking while using a mobile phone. Step length was reduced, while stance duration increased under reverse and dual-task conditions. These changes indicate a slower and more cautious gait strategy to maintain balance.

B. Joint Kinematics

Hip, knee, and ankle range of motion were reduced during reverse walking compared to forward walking. The reduction was greater during reverse walking while using the phone. This suggests restricted limb movement to improve stability during dual-task gait.

C. EMG Analysis

EMG recordings showed increased gastrocnemius activation during reverse walking compared to forward walking. During reverse walking with mobile phone use, muscle activation increased further.

D. Ground Reaction Force

Ground reaction force analysis showed smoother and lower peak loading during reverse walking. Participants showed more cautious loading patterns during dual-task walking.

E. Discussion

Reverse walking requires greater neuromuscular control than forward walking, and the addition of a cognitive task further increases gait difficulty. These findings support dual-task reverse walking as an effective method for assessing balance control and gait stability.
